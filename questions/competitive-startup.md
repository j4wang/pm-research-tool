# Competitive Research Questions - Startups


## Questions

# Leadership
1. What are the backgrounds of the company founders for each competitor (past companies and industries, track record, special expertise)?
2. What are the backgrounds of key leaders for each competitor (past companies and industries, track record, special expertise)?

# Funding & Investor signals
3. What is the funding history for each competitor (rounds, amounts, lead investors, most recent round date)? What does their funding stage and valuation suggest about their growth trajectory and runway?
4. Who are the lead investors, and what does their portfolio suggest about the strategic direction they're pushing? (e.g. a16z vs. a deep industry-specific fund signals different things)
5. Have any notable angels or advisors been announced, and what domain expertise do they bring?

# Traction and validation

6. What evidence of product-market fit exists — customer counts, revenue ranges, notable logos, or waitlist signals?
7. Are there any published case studies, testimonials, or press mentions that reveal which use cases are landing best?

Team composition

8. What does the overall team composition signal — are they engineering-heavy, sales-heavy, or domain-expert-heavy?
9. Are there notable hires or departures in the last 12 months?

Early market signals

10. What does the company's job posting activity suggest about where they're investing?
11. Are they showing up at specific conferences, communities, or publications that signal their target customer?

## Output format
<!-- Describe how you want the research brief structured -->
Produce a structured competitive brief with an executive summary, a competitor-by-competitor breakdown, and a final section on gaps and opportunities.
